Spicy Salmon Sushi Rice Bowl

Serves 4

Preparation Time Cooking Time Cuisine
15 Mins 20 Mins Japanese

Preparation Time: The time is takes to prepare and set the kitchen mise en place to begin cooking and is an estimate based on average home cooking skills

Cooking Time: The actual time it takes to cook the raw ingredients to be table ready!


Description

Build this spicy and speedy sushi-style salmon bowl in no time thanks to ready-to-heat brown rice. With sesame spinach, pickled cucumber and flaky pan-fried salmon, this easy meal is sensationally tasty.


Ingredients

The below ingredients are divided into the different cooking stages to help you organise on your worktop

Preparation
  • Mayonnaise - 60ml
  • Rice vinegar - 15ml
  • Cucumber - 1
  • Sriracha hot chili sauce - 8ml
  • Toasted sesame oil - 10ml
Garnish

These will be sprinkled over the top of your dish

  • Black sesame seeds - 5g
  • Spring onion - 2
  • Red Chili - 1
Cooking

These are the items you'll need when you get to the cooking stage of the recipe

  • 2 tablespoons of Canola or Sunflower oil
  • Fresh Salmon - 200g or 4 fillets
  • Spinach - 80g
  • White long grain rice - 280g

Instructions

This recipe being a cold rice bowl has only a preparation stage and plating stage. With many sauces, pickle and garnish options to choose from. So we'll make each one by one. The ingredients that need cooking is part of that preparation stage so we'll start with that!


Preparation

Cooking
Salmon
  1. Heat a pan and add your oil
  2. Once hot, add your salmon fillet skin side down, and cook for 10 minutes; skin side down
  3. Your fish is cooked when it turns opaque and flakes easily
  4. Remove from pan and set aside on a plate to cool down
Rice
  1. Add rice to a pot with 100ml of cold water
  2. Bring to a boil uncovered
  3. Once boiling, turn down heat and cover with a small vent
  4. Cooked in 12 mins, once cooked, switch off heat and fluff with a fork
  5. Set aside
Spinach
  1. Boil a kettle
  2. Wash your spinach and add to a colander
  3. Carefully pour the boiled water over the spinach so it starts to wilt
  4. Once wilted, pour under cold running water
  5. Squeeze out any excess water
  6. Add to a bowl with your toasted sesame oil and a pinch of salt
  7. This is your sesame spinach, place on your serving area as a topping
Preparing

This stage we prepare our garnishes

Red Chilli
  1. Slice your red chili finely
  2. Set aside in a bowl in your serving area as a topping
Spring Onion
  1. Slice your spring onion finely
  2. Set aside in a bowl in your serving area as a topping
Cucumber
  1. Chop your cucmber into small cubes and put in bowl
  2. Add a pinch of salt
  3. Add your rice vinegar to the bowl and mix up
  4. This is your pickled cucmber. Place on your serving area as a topping.
Salmon
  1. Flake your pre-cooked salmon into bitesized pieces by mashing with a form
  2. Set aside on serving area.
Sriarcha Mayo
  1. Combine your mayo and Sriarcha into a small bowl
  2. This is your sriarcha mayo. Set aside as a drizzle.

Plating

This stage we plate up!

Build your plate in the following order

  1. Rice
  2. Spinach to one side
  3. Cucumber to one side
  4. Top with Chili
  5. Top with Spring Onion
  6. Sprinkle black sesame seeds all over
  7. Drizzle with sriacha mayo
